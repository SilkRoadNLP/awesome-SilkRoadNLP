# Other Iranian Languages — NLP Resources

[← Back to main list](../README.md)

Several Iranian languages remain largely absent from computational linguistics research due to insufficient data availability. This page tracks the limited resources and emerging work for these under-represented languages.

---

## Languages Covered

- **Balochi** — Spoken by ~10 million people across Pakistan, Iran, and Afghanistan
- **Luri** — Spoken by ~5 million people in western Iran
- **Ossetic** — Spoken by ~500,000 people in North and South Ossetia
- **Gorani** — Spoken in parts of Iraqi Kurdistan and western Iran
- **Kashmiri** — An Indo-Aryan language with Iranian influences
- **Sindhi** — An Indo-Aryan language included in some Iranian-adjacent script normalization work

---

## Script Normalization (Multi-language)

| Paper | Authors | Year | Links |
|-------|---------|------|-------|
| [Script Normalization for Unconventional Writing of Under-Resourced Languages in Bilingual Communities](https://aclanthology.org/) | Ahmadi et al. | 2023 | [![GitHub](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Code-black?logo=github)](https://github.com/sinaahmadi/ScriptNormalization) [![HuggingFace](https://img.shields.io/badge/🤗-Demo-yellow)](https://huggingface.co/spaces/SinaAhmadi/ScriptNormalization) |

> This work covers script normalization for Perso-Arabic scripts of several languages including Gorani, Kashmiri, and Sindhi. The authors note that Luri, Balochi, Shina, and Burushaski could not be included due to lack of available data. Machine translation data could only be found for Kashmiri, Sorani, and Sindhi.
