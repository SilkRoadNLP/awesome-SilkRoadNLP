# Kurdish — NLP Resources

[← Back to main list](../README.md)

Kurdish is spoken by approximately 30 million people and functions as a bi-standard language: the **Sorani** dialect is written in an Arabic-based alphabet, while **Kurmanji** is written in a Latin-based alphabet. This dual writing system presents unique computational challenges for NLP.

## Lexical Resources

| Paper | Authors | Year | Links |
|-------|---------|------|-------|
| [Semi-Automatic Development of KurdNet, The Kurdish WordNet](https://aclanthology.org/) | Aliabadi | 2014 | [![GitHub](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Code-black?logo=github)](https://github.com/klpp/kurdnet) |
| [Towards Building KurdNet, the Kurdish WordNet](https://aclanthology.org/) | Aliabadi et al. | 2014 | [![GitHub](https://img.shields.io/badge/GitHub-Code-black?logo=github)](https://github.com/sinaahmadi/kurdnet) |

> **KurdNet** is the first prototype of a Kurdish WordNet, addressing the main challenges in building a lexical database for Kurdish including its inherent diversity and morphological complexity. The current prototype covers the Sorani dialect, with plans to extend to Kurmanji and other varieties.
